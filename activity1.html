Activity 1: Tidal Power Plant

Source of Energy

The tidal power plant harnesses the energy from ocean tides, which are caused by the gravitational pull of the moon and sun on Earth's waters. This renewable energy source is predictable and reliable, unlike solar or wind, as tides follow lunar cycles.

Conversion Process

The conversion process involves tidal turbines placed in coastal areas where water levels fluctuate significantly. As tides rise and fall, water flows through turbine blades, spinning them to generate mechanical energy. This mechanical energy is then converted into electrical energy via a generator connected to the turbine shaft.

Output/Utilization

The output is clean electricity that can be fed into the power grid or used locally in coastal villages. Tidal power plants are particularly suitable for regions with high tidal ranges, such as the Bay of Fundy in Canada or the Severn Estuary in the UK.

Real-World Application Relevance

In real-world applications, tidal energy contributes to reducing carbon emissions and provides a stable energy supply for coastal communities. For instance, the Sihwa Lake Tidal Power Station in South Korea generates enough power for 500,000 households, demonstrating the scalability and environmental benefits of this technology.

Overall, tidal power plants offer a sustainable solution for coastal energy needs, integrating seamlessly with marine environments while minimizing ecological impact through careful site selection and design.
