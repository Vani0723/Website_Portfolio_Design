Activity 2: Biomass-Based Biogas System

Source of Energy

The biomass-based biogas system utilizes organic waste from sources like cow dung in dairy farms as the primary energy source. This renewable resource is abundant in agricultural settings and provides a sustainable alternative to fossil fuels.

Conversion Process

The conversion process begins with anaerobic digestion, where microorganisms break down organic matter in the absence of oxygen within sealed tanks. This process produces biogas, primarily methane and carbon dioxide, along with digestate as a byproduct. The biogas is then purified and stored for use.

Output/Utilization

The output includes clean biogas for cooking, heating, or electricity generation through gas engines, and nutrient-rich digestate that serves as organic fertilizer. For a dairy farm with 100 cattle, this system can process approximately 2-3 tons of manure daily, yielding enough biogas to meet the farm's energy needs.

Real-World Application Relevance

In real-world applications, such systems are widely implemented in rural areas and agricultural communities. For example, India's National Biogas Programme has installed over 4.5 million biogas plants, reducing reliance on firewood and improving sanitation. This technology not only generates renewable energy but also addresses waste management challenges, preventing environmental pollution from untreated manure.

Overall, biomass biogas systems offer a circular economy approach, converting waste into valuable resources while promoting sustainable farming practices and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
